🐠 Key Largo Day Trip

~1 hr south of Miami · The Florida Keys start here

Key Largo is the diving capital of the world — first in the Florida Keys chain, easiest from Miami. Leave North Miami around 9:30–10AM, you're in the Keys by 11. The vibe shift is immediate: slower, saltier, more tropical. Full list of what to do — vote on your picks and lock in what you want.
On The Water — Top Picks
🤿
John Pennekamp Coral Reef State Park Top Pick
The first underwater state park in the US. Snorkel tours depart straight from the park out to living coral reef — barracudas, moray eels, sea turtles, vibrant formations. July water temps hit 85–88°F with 60+ ft visibility. The most iconic Key Largo experience and completely worth it for first-timers.
📍 MM 102.5, Key Largo · Snorkel tour ~$35–40/pp · ~2.5 hrs on water
⛵
Private Boat Charter
Rent a private boat with captain for a fully custom day — snorkel the reef, hit a sandbar, crack drinks in the open water, maybe spot dolphins or manatees. Birthday energy on the water with no strangers. Multiple operators: KL Boat Charters, Sea-EO, Key Largo Adventures. BYO drinks — they provide the cooler.
📍 Key Largo Marina · ~$400–700 for the boat (split 4 ways) · Half or full day
🌺
Tiki Boat Cruise
A floating tiki bar with a captain — bring your drinks, cruise the backcountry waterways, hit a sandbar. Fits 6 people max on the small boat, up to 28 on the party version. Cruisin Tikis and Key Largo Tiki Tours both operate here. Ridiculous and fun in the best way.
📍 Key Largo · 2 hr cruise · From ~$300 for the boat
🪟
Glass Bottom Boat — Key Largo Princess
75-ft motor yacht with a panoramic glass floor — you see the reef without getting wet. Sip cocktails on the sundeck while the reef passes underneath. Best midday when sunlight hits the coral. Good option if someone in the group isn't feeling the swim.
📍 John Pennekamp Park Marina · ~$35/pp · 2.5 hrs
🚢
The African Queen
The actual steamboat from the 1951 Katharine Hepburn / Humphrey Bogart film — docked and operating in Key Largo. Canal cruises and dinner cruises available. More of a quirky, interesting thing to see/do than a thrill activity, but genuinely cool if you appreciate the history.
📍 MM 99.7, Key Largo · Canal cruise ~$59/pp · 1 hr
Eat + Drink
🦞
Key Largo Fisheries Top Pick
They've been unloading fishing boats directly into their kitchen since 1972. Florida lobster, stone crab, mahi, shrimp — as fresh as it gets anywhere in the Keys. Casual waterfront setting, nothing fancy, just excellent fish. Perfect fuel stop before or after a water activity.
📍 1313 Ocean Bay Dr, Key Largo · Casual · Outdoor waterfront
🍹
Snapper's Waterfront Bar
Classic Keys waterfront bar — 3 different bars, sunset views, live music on Fridays and Saturdays, cold drinks. The kind of place where you sit down for one drink and end up staying for three hours. Locals and tourists mixed, zero pretension.
📍 139 Seaside Ave, Key Largo · Casual bar · Live music Fri & Sat

Miami Weather

July 2–5, 2026 · Typical Forecast

☀️ Miami in July: Morning sunshine, heat building to 90°F+ by afternoon, and near-daily thunderstorms that usually blow through in under an hour then clear completely. Humidity is real — you will sweat. Mornings are prime outdoor time. Ocean is ~85°F. Bring SPF 50+, UV is extreme every day. Pack a small poncho — afternoon downpours clear fast but they hit suddenly.
